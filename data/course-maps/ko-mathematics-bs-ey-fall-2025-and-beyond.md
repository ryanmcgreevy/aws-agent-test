## Pathway Summary
This pathway is a four-year Bachelor of Science in Mathematics plan for Indiana University Kokomo. The plan starts with foundational calculus, communication, laboratory science, and general education requirements. It then introduces linear algebra, physics, statistics, and upper-division mathematics choice groups. The upper years emphasize advanced mathematics sequences, science electives outside mathematics and statistics, internship or practicum planning, and career or graduate-school preparation.

## Program Description
The Bachelor of Science degree in Mathematics is designed to prepare individuals to understand the nature of truth and the concept of proof in the discipline of mathematics, to understand the application of mathematical techniques to other fields, and to formulate and solve problems mathematically. The Bachelor of Science places a greater emphasis on mathematical knowledge and its relation to the sciences through additional coursework and potential research opportunities.

## Canonical Plain-Text Passage
KO- Mathematics BS - EY Fall 2025 and Beyond is a public template mathematics pathway for Indiana University Kokomo under program KO-Mathematics BS-UGRD. The pathway spans four years and eight active terms and aligns with KO General Education and KO Degree Requirements. Year 1 Fall includes MATH-M 215 Calculus I, a Freshman Seminar, a Lab Courses requirement, a Written Communication requirement, a 120 Credit Hours for Degree Completion slot, and an advising milestone to meet with an academic advisor about major and career interests. Year 1 Spring includes MATH-M 216 Calculus II, Social and Behavioral Ways of Knowing, Speaking and Listening, a 120 Credit Hours for Degree Completion slot, and a career preparation milestone to create a resume and complete a University Job Board profile. Year 2 Fall includes ENG-W 221 Sophomore Writing Lab, a linear algebra requirement satisfied by MATH-M 301 Linear Algebra and Applications or MATH-M 303 Linear Algebra for Undergraduates, a physics requirement satisfied by PHYS-P 201 General Physics 1 or PHYS-P 221 Physics 1, a 120 Credit Hours for Degree Completion slot, and a milestone to plan engaged learning experiences. Year 2 Spring includes MATH-M 311 Calculus 3, MATH-K 310 Statistical Techniques, Ethically Responsible Citizenship, Humanistic and Artistic Ways of Knowing, a 120 Credit Hours for Degree Completion slot, and a milestone to plan a relevant internship or practicum experience. Year 3 Fall includes a Choose one of the following requirement, Diversity, Group A complete two sequences, Group B, a 120 Credit Hours for Degree Completion slot, and a milestone to complete a relevant first internship, clinical, or practicum this year. Year 3 Spring includes a 12 Credit Hours from Group A or B requirement that cannot use Group A courses already counted elsewhere, a 120 Credit Hours for Degree Completion slot, Electives in the School of Sciences but not Mathematics/Statistics, Group A complete two sequences, and Social and Behavioral Ways of Knowing. Year 4 Fall includes a 12 Credit Hours from Group A or B requirement, multiple 120 Credit Hours for Degree Completion slots, Group A complete two sequences, and a milestone to request commitments from references or recommenders. Year 4 Spring includes a 12 Credit Hours from Group A or B requirement, additional 120 Credit Hours for Degree Completion slots, Group A complete two sequences, a Non-lab Courses requirement, and a milestone to apply for full-time positions or graduate or professional programs.
